Modern Muar

Today, Muar is known as a royal town in southern Johor, which is rich in heritage and culture. Despite Batu Pahat overtaking it as Johor's second-largest town, Muar administratively remains the second most significant city, following the state capital, Johor Bahru. Muar District is one of 10 districts in Johor Darul Ta'zim, Malaysia. The district is located on the northwest side of Johor, bordering Tangkak District in the North, Segamat District in the East, and Batu Pahat District in the South. Among the most important cities in the district are the city of Maharani, the royal city of the state, and Pagoh.

The Muar District has a total area of 2346.12 square kilometres and a population of 328.695 people (Banian 2000). The declaration of Maharani City as the royal city of Johor was completed by the Sultan of Johor, Sultan Ibrahim, on February 5, 2012.3] It has a population of 110,000 and opened around 1884. and It was named after the title of the wife of Sultan Abu Bakar Daeng Ibrahim, Sultanah Fatimah Abdullah, namely Maharani.
