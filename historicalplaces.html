SULTAN IBRAHIM JAMEK MOSQUE

The Sultan Ibrahim Jamek Mosque is a stunning architectural marvel that combines Western and Eastern styles in Muarâ€™s signature blue and white colours. Dating back to 1930, it is situated by the Muar River and adjacent to Tanjung Emas Park. This mosque features a tall ceiling and a large prayer hall. Its minarets and mansion-like facade make it a must-visit landmark.

COLONIAL ARCHITECTURE

Muar, established as a modern town in 1885, boasts an array of prewar buildings that reflect a fusion of British colonial and Middle Eastern architectural influences. These structures offer a glimpse into the town's rich history and cultural amalgamation.

SULTAN ISMAIL BRIDGE

The Sultan Ismail Bridge, built in the 1960s, connects Muar to Tanjung Agas. Spanning nearly 400 meters, this bridge replaced the earlier ferry services. Although primarily functional, the bridge lights up at night, offering a picturesque view over the river. Visitors can enjoy these views from various points along the riverfront.
